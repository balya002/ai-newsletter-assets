✨ Set Your Default Email Tone with Copilot Draft Instructions
Copilot now lets you control how it writes emails by default using Draft Instructions. Instead of adjusting tone for every draft, you can define your preferred style once and have it applied automatically.
Draft Instructions in Copilot
You can guide tone, structure, and level of formality so drafts feel more natural and closer to your own voice.
Example:
I prefer communication that feels natural and not overly polished.

Write in a concise, natural, and friendly tone. Avoid overly formal or corporate language. Keep sentences short and easy to read. Get to the point quickly and avoid filler or repetition. Sound confident and friendly but not aggressive.
Note: Accessing Draft Instructions may look slightly different depending on your Outlook version.

Ready-to-copy Copilot prompts you can use immediately.
📌 Template #1: “Pull ESPN Sports Offerings from SharePoint”

Use case: Quickly finding internal materials on a specific offering or sales topic

I want to learn more about our ESPN sports offerings. Pull the most relevant information from SharePoint and summarize it for me.

Include:
• Key offerings or packages
• Important selling points
• Any audience, sponsorship, or content highlights
• Links or file names for the most relevant source materials

Keep it concise and easy to skim.
📌 Template #2: “Client Meeting Prep from an Internal Deck”

Use case: Quickly getting ready for a client meeting using an internal presentation

I have a client meeting coming up and our internal team sent this deck. Summarize it for me and highlight what I should know before the meeting.

Include:
• The main story or takeaway
• Key selling points I can mention
• Important data points or stats
• Any questions I should be ready to answer

Keep it focused, concise, and client-ready.
📌 Template #3: “Draft This in My Tone”

Use case: Turning rough notes into a polished email that matches your Draft Instructions

Turn the notes below into a clear email draft using my Draft Instructions.

Keep it concise, natural, and easy to skim.
Avoid overly formal language and remove filler or repetition.

Notes:
[paste bullets or rough thoughts]
